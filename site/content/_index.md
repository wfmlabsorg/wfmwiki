## WFM Labs

<img src="WFM-Labs_Wiki.png" title="WFM Labs Community Site" width="400" height="400" alt="WFM Labs Community Site" />
**[WFM Labs](https://www.wfmlabs.org)** is an organization formed by workforce management professionals seeking to reinvent the next-generation approach to successfully operating contact centers. Principles include:

- **Collaboration:** WFM Labs is a collection of workforce management projects developed by members of WFM Labs.
- **Adoption and remixing:** WFM Labs tests new ideas, processes, and creative approaches to be used by anyone seeking to improve their workforce management practices. Most organizations have unique requirements, so adoption and remixing of material are encouraged.
- **Transparency:** Anyone can inspect our project for errors or inconsistencies—transparency matters for our community.

This site is an extension of [Kyōdō Solutions](https://www.kyodosolutions.com/), [wfmlabs.org](https://wfmlabs.org) and our community site [community.wfmlabs.org](https://community.wfmlabs.org). Our wiki seeks to share practices and principles on topics including:

1.  **[[future-wfm-operating-standard]]** - creating the next-generation WFM playbook & re-examining all [[wfm-processes]] with an employee-first mindset
2.  **[[wfm-processes-monte-carlo-simulation-risk-modeling|Resilient Capacity Plans]]** - incorporating variance, volatility and the [[wfm-labs-risk-score]] system
3.  **[[wfm-processes-forecasting-attrition-employee-retention-and-turnover-models|Attrition Models]]** - applying WFM forecasting practices to solve the legacy problem of high employee turnover
4.  Leveraging the [[wfm-labs-maturity-model|**WFM Labs Maturity Model™**]] to benchmark and advance your WFM practices.

Join us as we collectively seek to advance the [[workforce-management-standard-introduction-contact-centers-and-traditional-wfm-approach|legacy practices of workforce management]] and advance our practices up the [[wfm-labs-maturity-model]]
